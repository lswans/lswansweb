About Me

I am a senior at the University of Washington, graduating with a B.S Earth and Space Sciences and Informatics in the Spring of 2023. In the Denolle Quake Lab, I am developing a web-hosted interface connected with the UW SERMEQ servers to allow streamlined, public access to 50TB+ of seismic DAS (Digital Acoustic Sensing) measurements from Washington, Canada and Alaska.

Upon graduation, I am interested in working with satellite imagery to observe natural phenomena, measuring useful data and packaging that data for consumer use. Applications that come to mind include satellite imagery for earth observation, cell network development, aerospace data engineering, etc.

My Projects

Denolle Quake Lab; Web Interface with Earthquake Database

Currently building an online interface with the UW Department of Earth and Space Science's seismic data server to allow public access to 50TB+ of earthquake measurements. Writing scripts and designing website to visualize said data in a useful manner.

ClarityAPI: Air Quality Web App

An air quality visualisation app that provides health recommendations based on your area's air quality readings.

Project VESPER: NASA RASC-AL Proposal

A proposal detailing a manned flyby mission of Venus to collect evidence of liquid water's past or present existence on the surface.

Project GLACIER: NASA L'SPACE Mission Concept Academy Deliverable

Geological Lunar Analysis of Crater-Ice for the Extraciton of Resources (GLACIER). A PDR (Preliminary Design Review) submission detailing a combined satellite and lander mission to quantify ice volume in the polar regions of the Moon.

Washington NASA Space Grant; Quantifying Climate Change Trends

A research project querying and visualizing 15+ years of sea surface temperature data, collected by thermal imaging satellites owned and operated by NASA Goddard Space Flight Center. The project focused on the Kuroshio Current, specifically the section of water surrounding Green Island.
